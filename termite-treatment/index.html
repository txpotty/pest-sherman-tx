Advanced Treatment Methods

Sherman Pest Pros utilizes the latest and most effective termite treatment methods available. Our approaches are designed to target termites at their source and create a protective barrier around your property. Some of the methods we may employ include:

Liquid Termiticide Barriers

Creating a treated zone around the perimeter of your home is a common and highly effective method. Liquid termiticides are applied to the soil around the foundation, forming a barrier that termites cannot penetrate without coming into contact with the treatment. Modern termiticides are non-repellent, meaning termites won't detect the treatment and will unknowingly travel through it, carrying it back to the colony and eliminating it. Termite Baiting Systems

Baiting systems offer an alternative or supplementary approach. These systems involve strategically placing bait stations in key areas around your property where termite activity is observed or likely to occur. The bait contains a slow-acting insecticide that termites consume and carry back to their colony, effectively eliminating the entire colony over time. Baiting systems are often less disruptive than liquid treatments and can be particularly effective for monitoring and controlling termite populations.

Wood Treatments

Swarming Termites

One of the most visible signs of a termite infestation is the presence of swarming termites, also known as swarmers. These are the reproductive termites that emerge from a mature colony to establish new ones. Swarming typically occurs during warmer months, often after a rain. You might see them flying around windows and doors or find piles of their discarded wings on windowsills or floors. Seeing swarmers inside your home is a strong indication that a termite colony is present, likely within the structure itself or very close by. While the swarmers themselves don't cause damage, their presence signifies a much larger problem: a developed colony actively damaging your property. If you observe swarming termites, particularly indoors, it is imperative to contact a pest control professional like Sherman Pest Pros immediately for an assessment and treatment plan. Ignoring swarmers is akin to ignoring the tip of an iceberg – the real threat lies beneath the surface. Prompt action can save you a significant amount of money and prevent extensive structural damage. This is a critical time to seek expert help for termite treatment. These winged insects are often mistaken for flying ants, so it's important to get a proper identification from a professional. Damage to Wood

Termites consume cellulose, the main component of wood. As they eat, they create hollowed-out areas within wooden structures, often leaving only a thin veneer of wood or paint on the surface. This damage can be difficult to detect visually from the outside. You might notice wood that sounds hollow when tapped, feels soft or spongy, or appears discolored or warped. As the damage progresses, the wood may crumble easily. Termite damage isn't always immediately visible, and it can occur in hidden areas within walls, floors, and ceilings. It’s important to be vigilant and inspect areas that are prone to moisture or earth contact, as these conditions are favorable for termites. If you suspect termite damage, avoid disturbing the affected area, as this can cause termites to abandon the area and make it harder to treat. Mud Tubes

Subterranean termites, the most common type of termite in Sherman, build mud tubes to travel between their underground nests and their food source (your home's wooden structures). These tubes are made of soil, wood particles, and termite saliva and are typically found on exterior walls, foundations, crawl spaces, and even inside your home on walls or ceilings. They serve as protected pathways for termites, shielding them from predators and dry environments. The presence of mud tubes is a definitive sign of an active subterranean termite infestation. If you find mud tubes, do not disturb them, as this can cause the termites to relocate and make treatment more challenging. Instead, contact a professional pest control service like Sherman Pest Pros for an inspection and treatment. Finding even small mud tubes is a serious indication that you have termites. Our experts understand the significance of these tubes and where to look for them, even in less obvious locations. These mud tubes are essentially highways for termites, allowing them to move between their underground colonies and the cellulose within your home. Disrupting these tubes without professional intervention can actually make the problem worse, as it forces the termites to create new, harder-to-find pathways. Frass (Termite Droppings)

Drywood termites, another type of termite less common but still present in some areas, don't build mud tubes. Instead, they push their droppings, called frass, out of small holes in the infested wood. Frass resembles small, hexagonal pellets and often appears as tiny piles near areas of termite activity. Finding these small piles of what looks like sawdust is a strong indicator of a drywood termite infestation. Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites live entirely within the wood they infest. Detecting their presence often relies on finding signs of their activity, such as frass and small kick-out holes in the wood. If you suspect you have drywood termites, it's important to contact a pest control professional who has experience treating this specific type of termite. Subtle Signs

Sometimes, termite infestations are more difficult to detect, with only subtle signs present. These can include:

  • Pest issues: While not direct signs of termites, recurring pest issues can sometimes point to underlying moisture problems that also attract termites.
  • Uneven or Bubbling Paint: This can indicate moisture trapped behind the paint, which can attract termites and also be a sign of underlying wood damage.
  • Hollow-Sounding Wood: Tapping on wooden structures and hearing a hollow sound can indicate that termites have been eating the wood from the inside out.
  • Excessive Moisture: Termites are attracted to moisture. Areas with poor ventilation, leaky pipes, or drainage issues can be hotbeds for termite activity.

If you notice any of these subtle signs, or have any general concerns about termites, it's always a good idea to schedule a professional termite inspection.
